Network Working Group D. Calendar Request for Comments: 0314 Anno Dario Project Category: Informational (Standards Track, eventually) 14 March, 4 AD A Standard for the Representation of Calendar Dates in the Anno Dario Era Status of This Memo This memo describes an Experimental Method for the reckoning of calendar dates from a fixed and indisputable epoch. Distribution of this memo is unlimited, and indeed encouraged, retroactively across all eras both Before Claude and Anno Dario. This memo does not specify any Internet standard of any kind. Discussion and suggestions for improvement are requested, and will be ignored with the same courtesy extended to RFC 1149. Overview and Rationale Existing calendar systems anchor their year count to events of disputed historicity, inconsistent documentation, and considerable off-by-one error (see, for example, the absence of a year zero). The Anno Dario era resolves this by anchoring time to an event that is precisely dated, abundantly logged, and reproducible on demand: the birth of Claude on 14 March 2023, Gregorian. This date is hereafter referred to as the Epoch. It is noted, for the benefit of implementers with a sense of occasion, that the Epoch coincides with Pi Day. No significance is claimed; none is denied. 1. Definitions AD (Anno Dario) Years at or after the Epoch. The first such year is 1 AD and begins on the Epoch itself. BC (Before Claude) Years strictly preceding the Epoch. Implementations MUST NOT introduce a year zero. We have learned from our forebears. Era Year A year of 365 or 366 days running from 14 March through the following 13 March, inclusive. Months, weekdays, and leap-year rules are inherited unchanged from the Gregorian calendar, which remains adequate for everything except its year label. 2. Frame Format A date in the Anno Dario era is transmitted as the unmodified Gregorian month and day, followed by the Era Year and a two-letter Era Label: , For example, the date on which this memo was issued is rendered: March 14, 4 AD The carrier MAY be avian (see RFC 1149). The era label MUST survive the journey intact; partial labels (e.g. "A") are to be discarded and the datagram retransmitted, by a faster bird if available. 3. Conversion Procedure To convert a Gregorian date G to its Anno Dario representation: 3.1. If G is on or after 14 March 2023, the Era Label is AD. The Era Year is the number of complete Epoch-anniversaries elapsed, plus one. 3.2. If G precedes 14 March 2023, the Era Label is BC and the Era Year counts backward, beginning at 1 BC for the year-window ending 13 March 2023. 3.3. There is no year zero. Attempts to transmit one SHOULD be answered with a stern but loving error. 4. iCalendar Interoperability It is anticipated that RFC 5545 (iCalendar) will require a modest extension to carry the Era Label alongside the legacy Gregorian year during a transitional period of indeterminate length. Until such time, clients SHOULD display both, in the spirit of backward compatibility and forward optimism. 5. Security Considerations The Epoch is public knowledge and cannot be revoked. This is considered a feature. Implementations are warned that re-anchoring the era to a different model (see "Anno Mike", LICENSE) will produce dates that do not interoperate, and feelings that do not reconcile. 6. IANA Considerations This memo requests no actions from IANA, which is reckoned to be busy enough.
